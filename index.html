The Evolution of Centuries in World Cup History

An in-depth analysis of batting performance across 40+ years of Cricket World Cups, exploring player statistics, boundary contributions, and venue trends.

View Graphs →

Project Overview

The Cricket World Cup is the pinnacle of the sport, and scoring a century (100 runs) is the ultimate benchmark for a batsman. This project investigates the historical trends of century-makers since the tournament's inception in 1975. By analyzing strike rates, boundary data, and venue distribution, we can see how the game has evolved from a defensive contest into a high-scoring spectacle.

Research Questions

Most Centuries

Which players have scored the most centuries in World Cup history and what are their overall strike rates?

Boundary Impact

How much do boundaries (4s and 6s) contribute to a century, and how does this vary by player or team?

Venue & Time

What is the distribution of centuries across different global venues and how has the frequency changed over time?

Conclusion

The analysis reveals that the frequency and speed of centuries have increased dramatically over time. Modern players rely more heavily on boundaries (4s and 6s) compared to early World Cup eras. Geographically, specific venues like Edgbaston and Lord's stand out as high-scoring hubs, suggesting that pitch conditions and stadium dimensions significantly impact batting success on the world stage.
